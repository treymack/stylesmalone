 Had we but world enough and time
This coyness, lady, would be no crime
We would sit down and think which way
To walk, and pass our long love's day
A hundred years would go to praise
Your eyes and on your forehead gaze
I'd spend 200 years upon each breast
And 30,000 go to the rest

But at my back I always hear
Time's winge'd chariot, hurring near
The grave is a fine and private place
But none, I think, do there embrace

So therefore while that youthful hue
Sits on your skin, like the morning due
Let us roll all our strength, and all
Our sweetness up into one ball
Let's tear our pleasures with rough strife
Through the iron gates of life
And though we cannot make our sun
Stand still, yet we will make him run


Adapted from Andrew Marvell's "To His Coy Mistress"
